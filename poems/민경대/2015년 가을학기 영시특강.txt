The first theme revolves around poetry itself, how it functions, and how it is read. The narrator (who stands in for Yeats himself) is asking the addressee (actress and Irish activist Maud Gonne who turned down several marriage proposals from Yeats):  When you are old and grey and full of sleep, And nodding by the fire, take down this book ... This introduces a theme found in several poems by Yeats that associate youth with poetic inspiration and old age as a time of reading and composing poetry and reflection on poems and their sources.    A second theme is the fleeting nature of life and love. He contrasts the most superficial love, that of those who loved the addressee for her beauty, with his own deeper and more spiritual love:   But one man loved the pilgrim soul in you, And loved the sorrows of your changing face ...  In the final stanza, both these themes join together in a suggestion that when she is old, as she reads these poems which express the deeper form of love, she will regret her earlier choices, for now, in old age, she will have the wisdom to see the difference between the two. 학생들의 레포트를 읽어가면서 깊어가는 가을밤을 보내며만감이 교차하는 시간에도 모든것은 벽을 담아가고 있다 세월의 등잔불을 타고 가는데이 가을 누구에게 편지를 보낼 사람도 없다 어리석은 자들의 행군만이 밤을 보낸다다시 책을 본다 이름을 본다 이름은 이미 나 자신으로 부터 사라진다 모든것은 하나의 허상이다 늙음은 하나의 허상의 복수구조이다
