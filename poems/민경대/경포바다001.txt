It has poetic history. The recent-ish history is traceable, the prehistory is imaginable. Wisely, Iain Sinclair took Allen Ginsberg there in 1967. William Blake had been there almost 200 years earlier, conversing with the Spiritual Sun. And then the Gorsseders, the bards, the druids, and Iolo. We wonder if Owen Pughe ever put in an appearance, or Richard Brothers. And what of the Shakespeare Tree…? Sinclair himself is Welsh-ish.
