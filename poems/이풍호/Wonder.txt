MODERN POETRY, no. 1, 1996 (ISSN 1089-1587) Wonder   - Joshua Tree National Monument, California, 1991    By Paul Lee  Joshua trees seemingly Live without a breath Under the scorching autumn sun, Praising God with a little move. The rocks lie immersed Into the cloudless azure sky.   Drops of the sky color the rocks, While a half moon voyages Above the Hidden Lake. What a day I wonder At His peace and quiet In a coy wilderness! -  이풍호
