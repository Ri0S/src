백제기행 (百濟紀行)  - 낙화암에서                          이풍호        삼천궁녀 몸을 던진    백화정(百花정) 그늘 아래     고란초 여린 잎은    살아있는 전설인가     저리도 푸른 백마강    연륜(年輪) 담고 흐르네.   발표일 1989. 9. 10. 백상시선 4 <사막의 달>/ 미주시조연구회사화집  Falling Blossom Cliff* By Paul Lee (이풍호) Under the shady Hundred Flowers PavilionWhere 3,000 court ladiesThrew their bodies, Are the young leaves of GoranchoThe living legend? Blue White Horse River flowsWith restless times. * Falling Blossom Cliff or Nahkhwaahm is the legendary cliff located in Puyo where this sad story happened in Paekche Kingdom, one of three old Korean kingdoms, vanquished by the Kingdom of Shilla and Tang China in 668. This poem was translated from the Korean Sijo 낙화암 written by the translator Paul Lee (이풍호, 1990. 10. 19).
